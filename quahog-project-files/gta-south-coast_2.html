01 / The Setup

Autumn 1986. Vinny Cabral comes home for one big import job, runs it through the New Sefton fish piers, and gets ambushed in the fog. The cash is gone. His crew is in the harbor. And a Providencia boss wants his half-million back. So Vinny does the only thing a man can do on the South Coast: he buys every mill, dock, bakery, and ferry until he owns the whole rotten coastline. Voiced protagonist · Empire-building loop · Take over the underworld one Asset at a time

02 / The Map

Four Districts,
One Dirty Coast

Three working towns split by water, bridges, and class, plus one battered city inland that runs on shoes and fistfights. Start broke in the mills, end up sipping somebody else's brandy on a yacht.

DISTRICT 01

Cascade

The Spindle City
// based on Fall River, MA

Seven brutal hills, abandoned brick textile mills, triple-deckers stacked to the sky, and the most Portuguese city in the country. Sweet bread, gold chains, Holy Ghost flags.

  • The Verde Bridge — rusting lime-green truss over the river
  • Dreadnought Cove — a parked battleship and a WWII sub
  • The Bordon House — notorious axe-murder B&B safehouse
DISTRICT 02

New Sefton

The Whaling City
// based on New Bedford, MA

The richest fishing port in America, and almost none of the money is clean. Cobblestone counting-houses up top; diesel, scallops, and union muscle down on the piers.

  • The Hurricane Barrier — a sea wall you can drive (in a storm)
  • The Leviathan Whaling Museum — laundering through "donations"
  • The South End — biggest Cape Verdean crew on the coast
DISTRICT 03

Cape Cobb

The Forearm
// based on Cape Cod, MA

A sandy arm of Old Money, reachable only by two permanently-jammed canal bridges. Yacht clubs with bloodlines, a Kennedy-esque compound, and dunes hiding the best smuggling beaches on the map.

  • The Burns & Sachem Bridges — chase missions that are really traffic
  • Hyena Port — the Camlin political dynasty's hedge-walled compound
  • The Fist — wild dunes and the most remote drops in the game
DISTRICT 04 · INLAND

Brockmore

The City of Champions · "Sole City"
// based on Brockton, MA

The one district off the water, twenty minutes up Route 24. A graveyard of giant brick shoe factories — once the Shoe Capital of the World — gone to the same rust-belt heartbreak as the Cascade mills. But Brockmore became Boxer Country: the toughest square mile of boxing in America, a place that turns laid-off factory kids into world champions. Empty enough to hide an army, proud enough to die for a statue.

  • Dead shoe factories — inland warehouses & bare-knuckle pits
  • Champion City Gym — the cleanest place in the game
  • The Champion's statue — disrespect it and the city comes for you
  • Route 24 — the fast, dark inland artery to the coast

05 / The Sidelines

Story Threads

Two deep optional strands, both pulled straight out of true South Coast history.

Cascade · The Highlands

Acquitted

// Lizzie Borden · Fall River · 1892–93

In 1892 someone took a hatchet to the tightest miser on Second Street and his wife. The city's one suspect was his unmarried daughter, Lizbeth. A New Sefton jury freed her in ninety minutes and the case was never solved. She bought the grandest house in the Highlands, named it Maplecroft, and lived out her days as the city's most genteel pariah. In 1986 the last of the line — ninety-one-year-old Vivian Bordon — becomes Vinny's untouchable patron on the Hill.

"It was never about being innocent, dear. It's about being acquitted."
Brockmore · City of Champions

The Undefeated

// a tribute to the 49–0 bloodline

Brockmore worships a shoe-worker's son who fought 49 times and never lost. His cousin Sonny still runs Champion City Gym — the one clean place on the map — with a Cape Verdean–Portuguese phenom one fight from a title. The Providencia mob wants a fix. You decide whose side you're really on. (A tribute to the real Marchegiano line.)

"We don't take a dive. We don't take their dollar. Forty-nine and oh, kid."
