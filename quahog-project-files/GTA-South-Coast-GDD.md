# GRAND THEFT AUTO: SOUTH COAST
### Game Design Document — Confidential / Internal Build "QUAHOG"
**Working titles:** *GTA: South Coast* · *GTA: Spindle City* · *GTA: Cranberry Coast*
**Setting:** Autumn 1986 → Summer 1987 · The South Coast of the Bay State
**Rated M for Massachusetts** — *Mature: Coarse Language (a lot), Strong Crime Themes, Reckless Operation of a Scallop Trawler, Aggressive Driving in Bridge Traffic*

---

## 1. TITLE & LOGLINE

> **GRAND THEFT AUTO: SOUTH COAST**
> *"The whales are gone. The mills are closed. The money's still here — you just gotta know which dock it washes up on."*

Autumn, 1986. **Vincent "Vinny" Cabral** comes home.

Eighteen years ago he left New Sefton a scalloper's kid with grease under his nails and a chip on his shoulder. He did a hitch in the Navy, drifted to Federal Heights in Providencia, and ran low-level errands for the **Provençal Family** until they trusted him with one real job: bring a freighter's worth of imported "product" up the coast, run it through his hometown connections, and disappear rich.

The deal goes down at midnight on the New Sefton fish piers, in a fog so thick you can taste the diesel. Then the floodlights cut on. By the time the gunfire stops, the money's gone, the product's gone, Vinny's crew is floating face-down in the harbor next to the bait barrels — and **Don Raimondo Provençal** is on the phone from Providencia wanting to know where his half-million dollars went.

Vinny survived. Vinny owes. And Vinny is going to find out who set him up if he has to buy every mill, dock, bakery, and ferry on the South Coast to do it.

He starts with nothing but a borrowed Members Only jacket, a rusted-out sedan, and a region full of people who'd sell their own grandmother for a quota share.

By spring, he's going to own the whole rotten coastline.

## 2. WORLD MAP & GEOGRAPHY — *The South Coast Translation*

The map is a single connected landmass split into **four districts** by water, bridges, and class: three working towns strung along the coast, plus one battered city inland. You start broke in the mills and end up sipping somebody else's brandy on a yacht. The drive from the bottom of the working coast to the top of the money takes about eight in-game minutes — and roughly forty years of social mobility.

> **THE COMMONWEALTH (in-game state):** "The Bay State," a.k.a. **the Cod State.** Motto on the welcome signs: *"You Should've Taken 195."*

### 🏭 CASCADE — *"The Spindle City"*
**Based on:** Fall River, MA

Built on seven brutal hills above a culverted river (the **Quequechan**, "the falling water"), Cascade is a vertical city of crumbling glory. Block after block of **abandoned red-brick and granite textile mills** — once the cloth capital of the world, now empty cathedrals full of pigeons, rust, and whatever the mob is storing this week. The slopes are stacked with **triple-decker houses**, three families to a building, laundry strung between them.

This is the most **Portuguese/Azorean** city in the country, and it shows: Holy Ghost flags, sweet-bread smell, gold-chained men arguing soccer outside the social clubs, the **Festa** crowds every August with the crown and the *sopas*.

- **The Verde Bridge** — a massive, rusting **lime-green cantilever truss** howling over the river (the *General Verde Memorial Bridge*; locals just say "the green bridge"). Tollbooth heists, motorcycle chases, and at least one mission where you drop a body off the top into the fog.
- **Dreadnought Cove** — a naval museum where the city parks a decommissioned **battleship (USS *Commonwealth*)**, a WWII **submarine**, and a destroyer. Late-game heist: hijack the battleship's deck guns. Yes, they work. No, you shouldn't.
- **Government Center** — a hideous brutalist concrete municipal slab where every permit, payoff, and rigged contract on the South Coast gets stamped.
- **The Bordon House** — a notorious "axe-murder" Victorian, now a tourist bed-and-breakfast and a perfect safehouse. The neighbors stopped asking questions a century ago.
- **Neighborhoods:** *The Flint* (Portuguese stronghold, social clubs, bakeries), *Corky Row* (rough, dense, loyal), *the Highlands* (faded mansions of dead mill owners), *Globe Village* (down by the water), *Below the Hill* (the docks and the chop shops).

### ⚓ NEW SEFTON — *"The Whaling City"*
**Based on:** New Bedford, MA

The harpoons are in the museum now, but New Sefton never stopped hunting — it just switched from whales to **scallops**, and quietly became **the richest commercial fishing port in America.** Money sprays off these docks like fish guts off a deck hose, and almost none of it is clean.

Up the hill: **cobblestone streets** and granite counting-houses in the **Historic District**, all whale-oil heritage and brass plaques. Down on the water: the **fish piers**, the **processing houses**, the diesel stink, the union muscle, and a fleet of draggers and scallopers where, in 1986, the most profitable thing in the hold often isn't seafood.

- **The Sefton Hurricane Barrier** — an enormous stone sea wall guarding the harbor mouth, with a rolling gate big enough to drive on. Drivable in calm weather; in a storm it's a wave-slammed deathtrap, which is exactly when missions send you across it.
- **The Whalemen's Chapel & the Leviathan Museum** — a seaman's church and a whaling museum full of scrimshaw, harpoons, and a full whale skeleton you can absolutely cause property damage to.
- **Fish Island & Pope's Island** — the harbor islands; ice houses, the co-op, the bait sheds, and the offshore-drop staging ground.
- **Clark's Point / Fort Taber** — a granite Civil War fort at the harbor mouth, the South End's pride, and a great spot to lose the cops or stash a boat.
- **Across the river: Fairhollow** *(Fairhaven)* — sleepier, churchy, with a fake-castle library; the place you flee to when New Sefton gets too hot.
- **Neighborhoods:** *The North End* (Portuguese/Madeiran), *The South End* (the largest **Cape Verdean** community in the country — its own food, its own music, its own crews), *The West End*, *Cobble Hill* (the cleaned-up cobblestone money).

### ⛵ CAPE COBB — *"The Forearm"*
**Based on:** Cape Cod, MA

A long sandy arm flexing out into the Atlantic — **bicep, elbow, forearm, wrist, and a fist** at the very tip. This is the **Old Money** of the **Codfish Aristocracy**: people whose great-great-grandfathers got rich off whale oil and cod and have spent the century since pretending they're just "summer folks." Pristine dunes, shingled compounds, yacht clubs with bloodlines, and a whole local economy of year-rounders ("washashores") who plow the snow and pour the drinks and quietly hate every Mercedes that crosses the canal.

Accessible **only by two bridges** over the **Cape Cobb Canal** — and both of them are a parking lot every weekend:

- **The Burns Bridge** *(Bourne)* and **The Sachem Bridge** *(Sagamore)* — twin steel arches, permanently jammed. Half the Cape's chase missions are really just *traffic* missions: weaving the breakdown lane, jumping the median, eating a $200 fine. There's an entire side activity built around the canal-bridge gridlock.
- **Hyena Port** — the gated, hedge-walled compound of the **Camlin family**, a Kennedy-*esque* political dynasty with a private dock, a touch-football lawn, and a Senator who runs the whole coast from the cockpit of a sailboat. (Fictional. Litigiously fictional.)
- **The Fist (Land's End)** — the wild tip of the Cape: great dunes, an artist colony, a tower monument, drag bars and dune buggies, and the most remote smuggling beaches on the map.
- **The Canal Stack** — a gigantic red-and-white striped power-plant smokestack you can see from anywhere on the Cape; a literal navigation landmark and a recurring drop signal.
- **Towns:** *Chathaven* (Chatham — lighthouse, gray-shingle wealth), *Sandtown* (Sandwich — oldest, the canal mouth), *Falconmouth* (Falmouth/Woods Hole — the **Woods Hollow Oceanographic Institute**, fresh off finding a famous shipwreck on the bottom of the Atlantic).

### 🥊 BROCKMORE — *"The City of Champions" / "Sole City"*
**Based on:** Brockton, MA

The one district that isn't on the water. Twenty minutes up **Route 24** from Cascade, inland and unloved, Brockmore is a city built on the two things the rest of the world stopped buying from America: **shoes and fistfights.**

Once the **"Shoe Capital of the World,"** Brockmore's skyline is a graveyard of enormous brick **shoe factories** — the same rust-belt heartbreak as the Cascade mills, just with the ghost-smell of old leather instead of cotton. Block after block of dead production floors, freight elevators, and faded painted signs for soles, heels, and lasts. By 1986 the work's gone overseas, the factories are empty enough to hide an army, and the only thing the city still manufactures is **champions.**

Because Brockmore didn't roll over. It became **"The City of Champions"** — the toughest square mile of **boxing** in America, a place that turns laid-off factory kids into world title-holders. This is **Boxer Country.** Every other corner has a gym; every gym has a kid who's getting out; every barstool has an uncle who "coulda gone pro if it wasn't for the hands."

- **The dead shoe factories** — your inland warehouse and chop-shop overflow, and home to the most brutal bare-knuckle fight pits on the map.
- **Champion City Gym (a.k.a. Marchegiano's)** — the sacred heart of Boxer Country and the hub of the *Undefeated* side story (see §7). The single cleanest place in the entire game.
- **The Champion** — a bronze statue of the city's patron god: the undefeated heavyweight king, a local shoe-worker's son who never lost a fight and died too young. Fists up forever at the top of the hill. People leave flowers. **Nobody disrespects the statue.** (The game will make you pay if you do.)
- **Route 24** — the long inland artery linking Brockmore to Cascade and the coast. Fast, dark, and the favorite stretch for late-night runs and high-speed chases away from the water.

### 🛣️ CONNECTING LANDMARKS (The In-Betweens)
- **Clayton Point Power Station** — twin **cooling towers** belching steam over the Cascade waterfront, visible from half the map, a classic getaway silhouette.
- **The Lighthouses** — Nauset-style, Chatham-style, the one on the breakwater — each one a flashing drop point. The beam sweep is a gameplay element: stay out of the light.
- **The Route 6 / Route 28 Strip** — a sad neon ribbon of **seedy motels** ("The Sip 'n' Sea," "The Mariner Motor Lodge"), clam shacks, mini-golf, a drive-in, and the **Donny's Donuts** drive-thru where every mission seems to start at 5 AM.

## 3. ART DIRECTION & ATMOSPHERE — *Coastal Fog & Working Neon*

Vice City was neon over palm trees and pink sunsets. South Coast is **neon over rust and salt** — the same 1986 dream, soaked, frozen, and three towns past hope.

### "Coastal Neon"
The signature look. Instead of glittering Art Deco, we light the night with what a fishing town actually has:
- **Flickering tavern and packie signs** — buzzing reds and blues over rain-slick **cobblestone** and oil-rainbowed **dock water.**
- **Lighthouse beams** raking through dense, salty **ocean fog**, catching the masts of the fleet.
- **Sodium-orange streetlights** humming on the triple-decker hills.
- The cold green glow of the **Verde Bridge** lights bleeding into the river mist.
- Wet brick. Wet asphalt. Reflections everywhere. The whole city looks like it's been crying.

Color palette: **gunmetal gray, rust orange, sodium amber, fog white, brine green** — with the Cape adding sudden bursts of **preppy pastel** (Nantucket red, kelly green, sky blue) like a different game entirely.

### 1986 South Coast Fashion
- **The Docks/Mills:** Heavy **Portuguese fisherman sweaters**, Carhartt and flannel, watch caps, work boots, **acid-wash denim**, **Members Only** jackets, **Starter** jackets (Sox, Pats, Celtics — it's the Bird era, people are insufferable about the Celtics).
- **The Gold:** Big, heavy **gold chains and crucifixes** — the Portuguese gold. The more you owe, the more you wear.
- **The Hair:** Aqua Net to the ceiling. Big feathered women's hair, mustaches on every man over twenty-two, the occasional rat-tail.
- **The Cape (a different species):** Popped collars, **boat shoes**, Nantucket reds, whale-print belts, cable-knit over the shoulders, Ray-Ban Wayfarers, the smug squint of inherited money.

### Weather Cycle (Dynamic, Brutal)
- **Nor'easters** — violent multi-day storms that **flood the low roads**, slam the hurricane barrier with whitecaps, knock out the sodium lights, and turn the canal bridges into white-knuckle crossings. Storm missions are a whole category.
- **Gray Raw Mornings** — the default. Forty-one degrees, drizzle, fog off the water, everything the color of a dead TV. Beautiful in a miserable way.
- **The "Gloria" Event** — a scripted hurricane (homage to the real **September '85** monster) that hits mid-game, tears the roof off a mill, sinks boats, and changes the map for several missions.
- **The Blinding Cape Day** — rare, glorious, summer. Cobalt sky, white dunes, packed beaches. Used almost ironically — the worst betrayals happen on the nicest days.

## 4. FACTIONS & THE UNDERWORLD

The South Coast underworld is a three-way knife fight between **old immigrant family loyalty**, **traditional Italian Cosa Nostra muscle from Rhode Island**, and **untouchable money in boat shoes.** Vinny will work for all three and betray all three.

### 🇵🇹 The Azorean Syndicate
*The home team. The dangerous-because-they're-family team.*
Deeply woven into Cascade and New Sefton — these aren't flashy gangsters, they're **uncles, cousins, godfathers, and the guy who runs the bakery.** Operations move contraband through the **commercial fishing fleet** and clean the cash through **legitimate businesses**: bakeries, social clubs, fish markets, the *Festa* committees. Fiercely loyal, allergic to outsiders, and impossible to flip because everyone is somebody's cousin.
- **Led by:** the **Medeiros family** — Tio "Manny" Medeiros, a soft-spoken patriarch who has fed half of Corky Row and buried the other half.
- **Sub-faction — The Crioulo Crews (New Sefton South End):** the **Cape Verdean** organization. Younger, tighter, controls the South End waterfront and the music-club money. Uneasy partners with the Azoreans, total enemies of the Providencia outfit. Their respect is earned the hard way.
- **Vibe:** church on Sunday, contraband on Monday. Cross the family, the whole neighborhood goes quiet on you.

### 🇮🇹 The Provençal Family *(Providencia LCN Offshoot)*
*The flash. The encroachers. The ones who set Vinny up.*
Traditional **Italian Cosa Nostra** pushing east out of **Federal Heights in Providencia** (next-state-over Rhode Island energy), trying to annex the South Coast's mills and unions. Slicked-back, big-collared, gold pinky rings, eating at the same restaurant on Atwood Hill every night.
- **Run by:** **Don Raimondo Provençal**, the legendary aging boss who "the Office" has feared for thirty years — and his hot-headed heir, **"Junior."**
- **Rackets:** illegal **gambling rooms hidden inside the abandoned textile mills** (dice, cards, sports books on the Sox and the Hagler fights out of Brockton), **loansharking**, and **union extortion** — squeezing the dockworkers and the longshoremen for a cut of everything that moves.
- **Vibe:** old-world rules, modern greed, zero loyalty to anyone who isn't from the Hill. They think the Portuguese are help and the Cape money is prey. They're wrong about exactly one of those.

### 🛥️ The Old Money & the Corrupt Bosses
*The real bosses. The ones who never get arrested.*
Two heads, one body:
- **The Union Bosses:** the **Dockworkers' & Fishermen's Brotherhood** — corrupt local leadership controlling who unloads, who fishes, and which crates get "missed" by the harbormaster. Crude, powerful, and sitting on top of the single most valuable smuggling chokepoint on the East Coast: the New Sefton piers.
- **The Codfish Aristocracy:** the **Camlin dynasty** and their Cape Cobb yacht-club set. Senators, "philanthropists," and trust-fund predators who launder fortunes through political donations and "art," set quotas to crush the small boats, and pull every string from the deck of a sailboat while never once touching anything illegal with their own hands.
- **Vibe:** the gangs kill each other on the docks; these people just change a law and ruin a thousand lives over cocktails. Final-act enemy energy.

## 5. PROGRESSION & ASSET PROPERTIES

Same beloved loop as Vice City: claw your way up, then **buy businesses.** Each Asset costs a pile of cash, generates **daily revenue** you collect in person, and unlocks a **strand of missions** to "make it profitable" (read: turn it into a crime engine). Own all five and the whole coast pays you rent.

| # | Asset | Location | The Front | The Real Business | Daily Take |
|---|-------|----------|-----------|-------------------|-----------|
| 1 | **Leviathan Scallop Co.** | New Sefton fish piers | Seafood processing plant | International smuggling — product packed in **scallop crates** under ten tons of ice | 💰💰💰 |
| 2 | **Quequechan Mill #4** | Cascade waterfront | "Vacant for redevelopment" | **Chop shop + illegal arms warehouse** in 200,000 sq ft of dead looms | 💰💰💰 |
| 3 | **Sachem Steamship Line** | Cape Cobb canal | Island ferry service | **Money laundering + offshore drops** on the Vineyard/Nantucket runs | 💰💰 |
| 4 | **Padaria Bela Vista** | The Flint, Cascade | Portuguese bakery & café | Cash-cleaning hub, message drop, and Azorean **made-man social club** in the back | 💰 |
| 5 | **The Leviathan Museum** | New Sefton Historic District | Maritime heritage museum | Laundering through "donations" and trafficking **stolen antiquities/scrimshaw** | 💰💰 |

### Asset Mission Strands

**1 — LEVIATHAN SCALLOP CO.** *(the engine of the whole game)*
- *"Bait & Switch"* — buy the plant off a union boss who's underwater on his loans.
- *"Cold Storage"* — clear the dock of a rival crew skimming product out of the ice room.
- *"Limit Out"* — bribe the harbormaster to "miss" your boats during the offshore drops.
- *"The Whole Catch"* — a midnight transfer at sea, ship-to-trawler, with the Coast Guard cutter closing in through the fog.

**2 — QUEQUECHAN MILL #4** *(the workshop)*
- *"Below the Looms"* — torch out the Provençal gambling room squatting in your new mill.
- *"Steal Order"* — fill a chop-shop wishlist: muscle cars, a fishing trawler's engine, and one (1) town plow truck.
- *"Heavy Industry"* — receive and hide an arms shipment that came in under a load of Portuguese floor tile.
- *"Spindle"* — defend the mill in a top-floor shootout while the LCN tries to burn it down around you.

**3 — SACHEM STEAMSHIP LINE** *(the launderette)*
- *"Bridge Toll"* — clear the canal of a competitor's boats so your ferry owns the route.
- *"Day Trippers"* — run dirty cash to the islands hidden in the luggage hold during a packed summer sailing.
- *"Dead Reckoning"* — a foggy offshore rendezvous where the buoy light is the only thing keeping you off the rocks.

**4 — PADARIA BELA VISTA** *(the heart, low-key the safehouse)*
- *"Sweet Bread"* — protect Tio Manny's bakery from a Provençal "insurance" shakedown. (Do this and the whole Flint has your back forever.)
- *"The Feast"* — a hit, a handoff, and a foot chase through the chaos of the August **Festa** — crowns, marching bands, ten thousand people, *sopas* simmering in vats.

**5 — THE LEVIATHAN MUSEUM** *(the prestige play)*
- *"Provenance"* — fence a stolen whaling-era treasure through a crooked "philanthropist" from Hyena Port.
- *"Endowment"* — launder a fortune through a black-tie museum gala packed with Camlin-dynasty money. This mission is your first real handshake with the people who actually run the coast.

## 6. VEHICLES & AUDIO

### 🚗 Vehicles (fictional makes, real-world misery)
- **The Triple-Decker** — an enormous brown wood-paneled station wagon the length of a triple-decker porch. Wallows, leaks, seats nine.
- **The Townie** — a beat-to-hell domestic sedan with a primer-gray door and a "MY OTHER CAR IS A DRAGGER" sticker. Vinny's starter car.
- **The Nor'easter** — a jacked-up 4x4 with a snowplow blade welded on. The single best vehicle in a storm and a fantastic battering ram.
- **The Açoreana** — a meticulously customized **lowrider**: hydraulics, candy paint, gold trim, a saint on the dash, and a sound system worth more than the car.
- **Mopeds & Scooters** — comically popular here. The **"Linguiça"** moped tops out at "downhill with a tailwind," but it threads alleys and bridge gridlock like nothing else. A core early-game traversal vehicle.
- **The Bourne** — a sad little economy commuter, spiritually defined by sitting motionless on a canal bridge.
- **Working Boats (pilotable):** the **Sea Dog** scallop **trawler** (slow, unsinkable, great for offshore work), the harbor **lobster boat**, the **dragger**, and the **Sachem ferry**.
- **Cape Money:** the **Codfish 40** cabin cruiser, the **Brahmin** sailing sloop, and the **Sou'wester** sportfisher with the fighting chair. All gleaming, all stolen beautifully.
- **Bonus background traffic:** the **Donny's Donuts** delivery truck, the **Bargain Basket** grocery van, town plows, the harbormaster's launch, and a rusted **Crown-Vic** cop car nobody respects.

### 📻 Radio Stations (4)

**1. WMILL 96.3 — "The Anvil"** · *Classic & Arena Rock*
Wall-to-wall **'80s arena rock** with a heavy hometown bias — the Boston-area legends (the harmonica-rock guys, the new-wave "Cars" crowd, the "more than a feeling" stadium boys) plus the national giants. Hosted by **"Iron Mike" Fontaine**, who has not been sober since the Bicentennial and dedicates every third song "to all the boys down the mill." The default station of every muscle car and dive bar on the map.

**2. RÁDIO ATLÂNTICO — WJFA 99.5** · *Portuguese Folk/Pop + Synthwave*
The soul of the Flint and the North End. **Traditional fado and pimba** colliding head-on with shimmering **'80s synth-wave** and Cape Verdean **morna** from the South End. Hosted entirely in a glorious Portuguese-English blend by **Zé do Rádio**, who reads out *Festa* announcements, soccer scores, funeral notices, and the occasional coded message between songs. Warm, homesick, and quietly the most powerful media outlet in two cities.

**3. WRGE 1480 AM — "The Rage"** · *Local Talk Radio (the comedy centerpiece)*
The most South Coast thing in the entire game. Host **"Buddy" Mello** screams about exactly five topics on an endless loop: **the fishing quotas** ("the gov'ment is killin' the little guy!"), **the bridge traffic** ("two HOURS at the Sachem, who's runnin' this?!"), **the weather** (full apocalyptic panic at the first snowflake — "BREAD AND MILK, PEOPLE"), **the mills closing**, and **the Patriots blowing the Super Bowl.** Callers named Donna from Corky Row and "first-time-long-time" Gary from Fairhollow phone in to make it worse. Genuinely useful, too: Buddy accidentally narrates the map's politics and tips off half the missions.

**4. WYAC 101.1 — "Yacht 101"** · *Yacht Rock / Light Classical (Cape Cobb only)*
Smooth, breezy, and quietly poisonous — the private soundtrack of the Codfish Aristocracy. Silky **yacht-rock** (the sailing-themed soft-rock crowd, the "smooth operator" set) and **light classical** for the cocktail hour. Hosted by **Chip Worthington III**, the only man on the entire South Coast who pronounces *every single R.* While the whole map drops them flat ("pahk the cah"), Chip leans into his — crisp, clipped, country-club **hard Rs** — and he is *performatively, insufferably proud of it.* He'll bring up "my hard R" unprompted, at the regatta, loudly, until the bartender and everyone within earshot quietly relocate to the far end of the dock. It reads as exactly the dog-whistle it is. Chip is a bigot in boat shoes, and the game frames him as the clown he is: he calls money "resources," the working towns "the unfortunate mainland," signs off each evening with *"…and remember, the canal keeps the riffraff south,"* and is — by a wide margin — the most satisfying man on the coast to launch off a pier. The joke is always on Chip. (See §8 for dialect direction.)

## 7. STORY THREADS — *The Sidelines*

Beyond the main empire war, two deep optional strands give the coast its soul. Both are built straight out of true South Coast history.

### 🪓 "ACQUITTED" — The Bordon Legacy
*Cascade, the Highlands · unlocks the Maplecroft safehouse*

In the summer of 1892, in a cramped house on **Second Street** in Cascade, somebody took a hatchet to the tightest-fisted miser on the block and his wife. The whole city had one suspect: his unmarried daughter, **Lizbeth Bordon**. In June 1893 she stood trial across the bay in the **New Sefton courthouse** — and after barely **ninety minutes** of deliberation, twelve men set her free. The murders were never solved. Lizbeth was the only person ever charged.

And she didn't leave town. She took her inheritance, bought the grandest house **up on the Hill in the Highlands**, named it **Maplecroft**, gave herself a finer first name, and spent the next thirty-odd years as Cascade's most genteel pariah — waving sweetly from her porch at the neighbors who crossed the street to avoid her, right up until they buried her in **Oak Grove Cemetery** a few plots from the very family she'd walked free for killing.

**1986:** Maplecroft still stands, and so does the last of the line — **Vivian Bordon**, ninety-one, sharp as a hatchet, the unofficial dowager-empress of the Highlands. She has buried four husbands ("all *natural*, dear") and outlived every enemy she ever made. She takes a shine to Vinny because she recognizes the look of a man the system can't quite pin down.

She becomes his patron on the Hill, and the thread's whole thesis is the **real rule of the South Coast** — the one she's lived by for a century: *it was never about being innocent. It's about being **acquitted.***

**Mission strand "The Maplecroft Files":**
- *"Forty Whacks"* — a discreet "removal" for Madame Bordon that earns you the run of Maplecroft as a safehouse. No questions. There are never questions.
- *"The Tour"* — stage a job during a cheesy true-crime ghost tour of the old Second Street murder house, now a morbid little bed-and-breakfast that sells hatchet keychains in the gift shop.
- *"Reasonable Doubt"* — muddy the evidence so cleanly that the cops *have* to let your man walk, Bordon-style. Vivian coaches you personally.
- *"Oak Grove"* — a midnight job in the family cemetery on the anniversary, **August 4th**, in heavy fog, with the whole Hill watching from behind their curtains and saying absolutely nothing.
- *"The Hatchet"* — the (alleged) original murder weapon resurfaces as a relic half the coast wants. Recover it for Vivian. She keeps it on the mantel. Of course she does.

### 🥊 "THE UNDEFEATED" — Brockmore's Bloodline
*Brockmore · the cleanest story in a dirty game*

Brockmore worships one man above God, the Sox, and the Pope: the **undefeated heavyweight champion of the world** — a shoe-worker's son from these exact streets who fought **49 times and never, ever lost**, retired with the belt still on his shoulder, and then died in a plane crash the night before his birthday and became immortal. His father stitched soles in a Brockmore factory by day; the son punched his way out of the leather and the poverty and put the City of Champions on the map for good. There's a statue. *You do not touch the statue.*

> **🛠 Designer's note:** This thread is a tribute, not a cash-in. The real champ — **Rocco Marchegiano**, known to the world as **Rocky Marciano** — went a perfect **49–0** and is stitched into actual South Coast blood. This storyline exists to honor that lineage. *(Nic — this one's for your family.)*

**1986:** the gym he came up in, **Champion City Gym**, still stands, run by his aging cousin **Sonny Marchegiano** — a leathery, big-hearted old cutman who came up alongside the champ, never took a dime from the mob, and is the one character in the game with genuinely clean hands. Sonny has a kid in the gym: **young "Sully" Tavares**, a Cape Verdean–Portuguese phenom out of New Sefton's South End with a glass-shattering right hand and a real, honest shot at a title — *if* the Provençals don't get to him first.

Because the Providencia outfit doesn't want a champion. They want a **fix.** Easy money in the fight books, easy leverage over a whole city, and besides — a clean gym is an insult to everything they run.

**Mission strand "City of Champions":**
- *"Sole City"* — meet Sonny, move your business *out* of his neighborhood (he won't let it within a block of the gym), and earn the old man's trust the hard way.
- *"The Fix Is Out"* — the Provençals order you to make Sully take a dive. You choose whose side you're really on. (Choosing the gym sets the back half of the entire thread.)
- *"Leather & Bone"* — a brutal chase and brawl across the floors of an abandoned shoe factory when the mob comes to break the kid's hands.
- *"Forty-Nine and Oh"* — protect Sully on fight night at the old Brockmore arena while the LCN tries to rig the count, the crowd, and the exits.
- *"The Statue"* — somebody desecrates the Champion's monument. You make it right, in public, with the whole city watching. Pull this off and Boxer Country is yours for good.

**The payoff:** keep the gym clean and the kid undefeated, and Brockmore becomes the one corner of the whole map that's *yours* — not because you bought it, but because you earned it.

## 8. VOICE ACTING & DIALECT DIRECTION — *"Pahk the Cah, We Got Wuhk"*

**Mandate:** the accent is not a garnish, it's a **load-bearing wall.** Every line, bark, and radio ad is soaked in **Eastern New England non-rhotic** — the South Coast / Boston habit of dropping every R and then sticking it back where it doesn't belong. When in doubt, lay it on **thicker.** This is the one department where the official direction is: *too much is the correct amount.*

### The Core Phonetics (apply to nearly everyone)
- **Drop the R after a vowel:** car → *cah*, park → *pahk*, hard → *hahd*, winter → *wintah*, water → *wadah*, here → *heah*, your → *yaw*, beer → *beeyah*.
- **Add an R where there isn't one (intrusive R):** idea → *idear*, "I sawr it," tuna → *tunah → tuner*, "we got pizzer down the cornah."
- **Broad A:** can't → *cahn't*, half → *hahf*.
- **The khakis test:** "Wheah's my khakis?" / "Next to ya cah keys." Use this at least once per build.

### The Lexicon (use relentlessly)
*wicked* (very — "wicked pissah"), *pissah* (excellent), *bubblah* (water fountain), *grindah* (a sub), *frappe / cabinet* (a milkshake), *the packie* (liquor store), *the rotary* (traffic circle), *down the Cape*, *bang a uey* (U-turn), *the Sawx / the Pats / the Celts / the Broonz*, *Dunkies*, *coffee regulah* (cream and two sugars), *so don't I* (this means "so do I"), *no suh*.

### Voice Notes by Character
- **Vinny Cabral (protagonist):** Cascade-via-Providencia. Dry, tired, fully non-rhotic. — *"Half a million bucks. Gone. In the watah, with my whole crew. So no, Don, I'm not havin' a great mahnin'."*
- **Buddy Mello (WRGE talk radio):** non-rhotic at maximum volume with zero filter. — *"Two HOWAHS at the Sachem this mahnin', two howahs! Who's runnin' this cahnal, a buncha clams?! And don't get me STAHTED on the quoter!"*
- **Tio Manny Medeiros:** soft Azorean-Portuguese laid over the South Coast drop. Warm, lethal. — *"Eh, Vinny. You eat? No? Siddown. We talk about ya problem aftah the sweet bread."*
- **Don Raimondo Provençal:** Federal Heights LCN, non-rhotic with old-world Italian gravel. — *"You lost my money on YA home turf, kid. Heah's how ya gonna pay it back."*
- **Sonny Marchegiano (Brockmore):** raspy old cutman, full City-of-Champions drop. — *"In this gym we don't take a dive, we don't take a dollah from those bums, and we don't lose. Fawty-nine and oh, kid. Fawty-nine and oh."*
- **Vivian Bordon (the Highlands):** the rare "refined" exception among the locals — clipped, old-money Yankee with velvet menace, *almost* as rhotic as Chip but a hundred times scarier. — *"I was **acquitted**, dear. Do learn the difference."*

### ⚠️ The Chip Worthington III Bit (recurring, satirical)
Chip is the **one and only character who keeps every R** — sharp, smug, deliberate **"hard Rs."** Where the entire coast says *cah*, Chip says *carrr* and makes a whole production of it. The running gag is that he is **performatively, obnoxiously proud of his hard R** and brings it up unprompted — and it reads as precisely the dog-whistle it sounds like. He's a country-club bigot; the game treats him as a contemptible buffoon; every decent character on the map physically edges away from him; and the punchline always, *always* lands on **Chip** — never on anyone he sneers at.

> **Direction:** imply, never spell out. The bit works *because* nothing is ever said outright. The joke is **his**, the disgust is **everyone else's**, and the pier is right there whenever the player's had enough.
